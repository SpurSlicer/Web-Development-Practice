History

Cave Story: A 2d platformer built for the windows operating system made by Diasuke Amaya following the adventure of the protagonist Quote as he travels through a mysterious island.
Release Date: 12/20/2004
Mediums: Cave story is playable on Windows as freeware, Windows via steam (Cave Story+), DSIWare, Wiiware, and Nintendo Switch. Note that DSIWare and Freeware are similar enough that distinction is not necessary for the scope of this encyclopedia.
Cave Story Wiiware: A reliscenced version of Cave Story originally produced for the Nintendo Wii managed by Nicalis. This version features 2x. upscaled graphics, challenges, alternate universe stories, difficulty modes, and a few other functionalities.
Release Date: 3/22/2010
Cave Story +: A modified release of Cave Story Wiiware with more challenges, features, and settings that is borderline unplayable due to neglagence with updates. Cave Story + is available through Steam, Epic, GOG, and the Nintendo Switch.
Release Date: 11/22/2011 (Steam); 6/20/2020 (Switch); 12/3/2020 (Epic); 12/17/2021 (GOG)

Plot

Setting: Cave Story takes place on a floating island in the sky held in the air with the magic produced by the core. On this island, there exist a group of creatures called mimiga that maintain a rabbit-creature like appearance. In addition, human scientists also existed on the island.Before the game takes place, a great war occurred between surface robots and mimiga--note this was not brought on by the scientists--that resulted in a mass extermination.
Mimiga Village: The last large-standing village on the island safe from the doctor's tyranny.
Egg Corridor: A scientific base created to study the incubation of dragon eggs.
Grasstown: An overgrown cave area home to several mimiga.
Sandzone: A sandy waste land home to Curly, several mimiga, Jenka who is a native witch on the island, and a garden of powerful red flowers.
Labryinth: A confusing cave system used by Misery, Jenka's daughter, to banish individuals.
Core Room: A decrepate scientific hub used for analyzing the islands core, a mysterious entity that keeps the island afloat.
Waterway: A flooded cave area responsible for routing water throughout the island.
Outerwall: A massive wall on the right-most side of the island--when viewing in 2d.
Plantation: A farm area where food was once harvested for the island. It is now used as a slave area for mimiga to farm red flowers under the control of a human named the doctor.
Balcony: The top of the island where helecopters can land. A palace resides here which is where the doctor resides.
Sacred Grounds: A hell-styled cave system where Jenka's brother, Ballos, was banished.
Characters: Here is a brief overview of important characters:
Quote: The main protagonist; quote is a silent robot that wakes up after the war. Quote did not fight in the war and sided with the mimiga alongside his friend, Curly.
Curly: A protagonist; curly is another robot that chose to side with the mimigas and help protect them. She was sent to the island with Quote but sided with the mimiga.
Sue: A protagonist; Kazuma's younger sister that was transformed into a mimiga.
Kazuma: A protagonist; Kazuma was a scientist on the island that found himself locked in a shed in Grasstown.
Booster: A protagonist; A scientist that worked closely with Kazuma.
Jenka: A protagonist; A powerful witch native to the island.
Ballos: The main antagonist; A powerful wizard native to the island that was sealed in a catacomb by Jenka, his sister.
The Doctor: An antagonist; A scientist studying the demon crown, a powerful relic giving the doctor powerful magic and controlling powers.
Misery: A protagonist; Jenka's daughter--who also possesses magical abilities--that is under the control of the demon crown via a curse.
Balrog: A protagonist; A mysterious robot with the apearance of a lunch box that is also under contorl of the demon crown.

Story: Quote wakes up and makes his way through the island with amnesia, meeting scientists that are looking for a way off the island. He learns that mimiga are being kidnapped by a dangerous individual known as the doctor for experiments to be done on them. When mimiga are fed red flowers, they become rabid and powerful and lose any sense of self they once had. Quote finds a witch and robot named Misery and Balrog, respectively that attempt to stop Quote in his tracks. Eventually, Quote finds another robot named Curly whom he was once friends with. Skipping some details, they eventually find that the doctor is behind the kidnappings and that Misery and Balrog are under the control of the demon crown, and they work to defeat the Doctor.

Programming

Game: Cave Story was created with windows native GUI programming and originally occupies an impressive 4kb despite containing 2-4 hours of gameplay. Cave Story was compiled to x86-32 assembly to run on windows machines and was coded in C++ despite primarily using C. Cave Story has its own stage format and encoding scheme for entities and was built entirely from the ground up. Cave Story was in several years of development as a passion project and has an infamous beta that differs widely from the game we received in 2004.

Modding

Engines: Modding Cave Story is a relatively simple process once you get your head around how the program works, but adding in native changes to the code is bound by memory constraints. However, recreations of the engine through projects such as CSE2, NX-Engine, and Doukutsu-RS exist to allow complete freedom to add in anything you desire.
